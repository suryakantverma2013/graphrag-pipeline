# ACME Model X200 Centrifugal Pump — Service Manual

The ACME X200 is a single-stage centrifugal pump used for water transfer in
industrial cooling loops. This manual covers installation, routine operation,
and field service of the X200 and its replaceable wet-end components. It is
intended for trained maintenance technicians familiar with rotating equipment.

## Installation

Mount the pump on a level concrete base and align the coupling to within
0.05 mm total indicated runout. Connect the suction line using the supplied
150 mm flange gasket and torque the bolts in a star pattern to 45 Nm. The
discharge line must include an isolation valve and a check valve rated for the
system working pressure of 16 bar. Verify the direction of rotation by bumping
the motor before connecting the coupling guard.

WARNING: Do not run the pump dry. Operating the X200 without process fluid for
more than 30 seconds will destroy the mechanical seal and may overheat the
bearing housing, creating a burn and fire hazard. Always prime the suction
line before the first start.

## Operation

During normal operation the X200 should run within its preferred operating
region between 40 and 110 cubic metres per hour. Monitor the bearing
temperature and the discharge pressure during the first hour of running. If
the pump cavitates, indicated by a gravel-like noise and fluctuating
discharge pressure, reduce the flow or increase the suction head until the
noise stops. Record the operating point in the equipment log at the end of
each shift.

### Routine Checks

Perform the following checks at the stated intervals:

- Daily: inspect for leaks at the seal and flange faces, and log bearing temperature.
- Weekly: check coupling alignment and verify guard fasteners are tight.
- Monthly: grease the bearings with 4 grams of the specified lithium-complex grease.
- Annually: replace the mechanical seal and inspect the wear rings for clearance.

## Service

Before any service work, isolate the motor at the local disconnect and lock
out the breaker. Drain the casing through the bottom plug and allow the unit
to cool. Replacement of the mechanical seal requires removal of the bearing
housing and the impeller; refer to the exploded diagram for the correct
reassembly order. After service, refill the casing, restore power, and verify
the rotation direction before returning the pump to service.
